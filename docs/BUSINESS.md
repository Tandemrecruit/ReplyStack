# Business Model & Market Analysis

## Target Market

### Primary: Local Service Businesses

**Profile:**
- 1-5 locations
- Receives 10-100+ Google reviews per month
- Owner or manager handles reviews (no dedicated marketing person)
- Values online reputation but lacks time

**Industries:**
- Restaurants and cafes
- Dental and medical practices
- HVAC and home services
- Salons, spas, and barbershops
- Auto repair and dealerships
- Legal and accounting firms
- Fitness studios and gyms
- Pet services (vets, groomers)
- Real estate agents

**Pain Points:**
- Know reviews matter but don't have time
- Feel guilty about ignored reviews
- Hate writing the same thing over and over
- Worried about saying wrong thing to negative reviews
- Can't afford reputation management agencies ($500+/month)

**Size of Market:**
- ~33 million small businesses in the US
- ~8 million have Google Business Profiles
- ~2 million actively receive reviews monthly
- TAM: $500M+ annually (at $29-79/mo avg)

### Secondary: Marketing Agencies

**Profile:**
- Managing 10-100+ local business clients
- Review response is a service offering
- Currently using VAs or doing manually
- Want to scale without adding headcount

**Pain Points:**
- Review response is time-consuming but low-margin
- Hard to maintain client voice across team
- Clients expect fast response times
- Need reporting to prove value

**Size of Market:**
- ~15,000 digital marketing agencies in the US
- ~5,000 offer reputation management
- Potential: 1,000 agency customers at $199/mo = $2.4M ARR

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors

| Competitor | Pricing | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|------------|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Podium** | $289+/mo | Full suite, SMS | Expensive, bloated |
| **Birdeye** | $299+/mo | Multi-platform | Enterprise-focused |
| **Reputation.com** | Custom | Enterprise features | Not for SMBs |
| **NiceJob** | $75+/mo | Review generation | AI is add-on |
| **GatherUp** | $99+/mo | Good reporting | Complex setup |

### Indirect Competitors

- **Manual response:** Owner writes each response (time-consuming)
- **Generic templates:** Copy-paste responses (obvious to customers)
- **Virtual assistants:** Hiring VAs ($500+/mo for quality)
- **ChatGPT directly:** Free but no integration, manual workflow

### Our Differentiation

1. **AI-native:** Not AI bolted onto legacy software
2. **Simple:** One job done well, not 50 features
3. **Affordable:** $29-79 vs $299+ competitors
4. **Voice matching:** AI actually sounds like the business
5. **One-click workflow:** See review → generate → publish

## Pricing Strategy

### MVP: Single Tier

**$49/month** (14-day free trial)
- 1 location
- Unlimited AI responses
- Google integration
- Email notifications

*Rationale:* Validate willingness to pay before optimizing pricing.

### Phase 2: Tiered Pricing

| Plan | Price | Locations | Responses | Features |
|------|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Starter** | $29/mo | 1 | 50/month | Core features |
| **Growth** | $79/mo | 3 | Unlimited | + Analytics, templates |
| **Agency** | $199/mo | 10 | Unlimited | + Team, API, white-label |

### Pricing Psychology

- **$29 Starter:** Low barrier, validates market fit
- **$79 Growth:** Main revenue tier, clear upgrade path
- **$199 Agency:** High-margin, supports multi-client use case

### Future Considerations

- Annual discount (2 months free)
- Per-location add-ons ($15/location beyond plan limit)
- AI response packs for Starter tier overages
- Enterprise custom pricing for 50+ locations

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Founder-Led Sales (Months 1-3)

1. **Waitlist building**
   - Landing page with value prop
   - LinkedIn posts about the problem
   - Indie Hackers, Twitter/X engagement
   - Target: 500 waitlist signups

2. **Early adopters**
   - Personal outreach to 100 businesses
   - Offer extended trial (30 days)
   - Weekly feedback calls
   - Target: 20 paying customers

3. **Case studies**
   - Document time saved
   - Before/after response quality
   - Get testimonials and screenshots

### Phase 2: Content & SEO (Months 3-6)

1. **Content pillars**
   - "How to respond to [industry] reviews"
   - "Google review response templates"
   - "Review response best practices"

2. **SEO targets**
   - "google review response tool"
   - "ai review response generator"
   - "how to respond to negative reviews"

3. **Social proof**
   - G2/Capterra listings
   - Case study library
   - Customer testimonial videos

### Phase 3: Paid Acquisition (Month 6+)

- Google Ads (search intent)
- Facebook/Instagram (awareness)
- YouTube pre-roll (demo videos)
- Retargeting (site visitors)

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Google API changes | Medium | High | Abstract API layer, monitor deprecations |
| AI cost increases | Low | Medium | Optimize prompts, cache similar reviews |
| Competitor price war | Medium | Medium | Focus on UX, not price |
| Low conversion rate | Medium | High | Iterate on onboarding, add trial |
| High churn | Medium | High | Focus on sticky features (voice profile) |
| Review policy changes | Low | High | Stay compliant, no automation violations |
